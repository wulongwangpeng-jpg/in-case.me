Most people guess they have 15 or 20 online accounts. When they actually count, the real number is three to four times that.

Every blank cell on this page is a question mark your family would face if you couldn't log in tomorrow. But each one you fill in turns a question mark into a plan.

## How to Use This

Go category by category. For each platform you use, mark the checkbox — then ask yourself: if something happened and I couldn't log in, would anyone know what to do?

The tables below spell out what happens in both directions. The gray text is fixed — that's what's at stake. The blank cells are yours.

## What to Do With the List

Once you've filled it in, you have options:

**Notebook.** Print it, seal it, tell someone where it is. Simple. Risk: anyone who finds it can read everything.

**Password manager.** Use emergency access (1Password, Bitwarden, Apple Legacy Contact). Risk: your contact needs their own account, and access delays vary.

**Encrypted vault.** Store everything in a system where encryption runs in your browser. Name someone you trust. If you stop checking in, they get access. If you're fine, nothing happens.
